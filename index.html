Sustainability Through the Ages

Explore Intel's journey through time, discovering how our commitment to innovation has shaped a more sustainable future for technology and our planet.

Intel Sustainability Timeline

1968

Intel Founded

Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore rename the newly formed company NM Electronics to Intel Corporation, laying the foundation for decades of technological innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel debuts the 4004, the world's first commercial microprocessor, igniting the microprocessor revolution and propelling the future of computing devices.

1978

8086 Processor

Launch of the 8086 processor, establishing the x86 architecture that drives countless PCs and servers in the modern era.

1985

386 Processor

Intel introduces the 386 processor with 32-bit architecture, ushering in a new era of performance and multitasking for personal computers.

2006

Peak GHG Emissions

This year marks Intel's highest annual greenhouse gas emissions for operations. Over subsequent years, Intel invests heavily in chemical abatement, renewable energy, and energy-efficient manufacturing to reverse this trend.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches its RISE (Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, Enabling) strategy and 2030 goals, aiming to drive industry-wide progress on climate action, water stewardship, and waste reduction.

2022

Net-Zero By 2040

Intel announces its commitment to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across its global operations by 2040, building on years of environmental initiatives.

2023

Renewable Electricity

The company achieves 99% renewable electricity usage worldwide, helping to drastically lower carbon emissions and driving progress toward Intel's long-term sustainability goals.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Intel hosts its first Sustainability Summit, uniting suppliers, government officials, and industry leaders to collaborate on next-generation sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.

Frequently Asked Questions

Intel is committed to achieving net-zero greenhouse gas emissions (Scope 1 and 2) across our global operations by 2040. This includes investing in renewable energy, increasing energy efficiency, and implementing carbon reduction technologies.

Intel conserves water through advanced recycling systems, water-efficient manufacturing processes, and restoration projects. We aim to restore more water than we consume, partnering with local communities to protect watersheds and improve water quality.

Our circular economy approach focuses on zero waste to landfill, designing products for longevity and recyclability, and increasing the use of recycled materials in our manufacturing. We aim to keep materials in use as long as possible and minimize environmental impact.

RISE stands for Responsible, Inclusive, Sustainable, and Enabling. This strategy guides our 2030 goals and includes commitments to climate action, water stewardship, waste reduction, supply chain sustainability, and creating technology that benefits everyone.

Climate Action Details

Our Commitment to Net-Zero

Intel has set ambitious goals to achieve net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2040. Our comprehensive approach includes:

  • Renewable Energy: Achieved 99% renewable electricity usage globally in 2023
  • Energy Efficiency: Investing in advanced manufacturing technologies to reduce energy consumption
  • Carbon Reduction: Implementing chemical abatement systems to reduce process emissions
  • Green Buildings: Constructing LEED-certified facilities worldwide

Measurable Impact

Since our peak emissions in 2006, we've reduced absolute greenhouse gas emissions by over 40% while growing our business significantly. This demonstrates that economic growth and environmental responsibility can go hand in hand.

Water Conservation Details

Restoring More Than We Use

Water is essential to semiconductor manufacturing. Intel is committed to being a responsible water steward by:

  • Water Recycling: Recycling up to 80% of water used in our manufacturing processes
  • Restoration Projects: Funding projects that restore nearly 9 billion gallons of water annually
  • Community Partnerships: Collaborating with local organizations to improve water quality
  • Smart Technologies: Implementing AI and IoT solutions to optimize water usage

Global Impact

Our water restoration efforts benefit communities around the world, helping to replenish watersheds, restore habitats, and ensure clean water access for future generations.

Learn About Water Projects

Circular Economy Details

Zero Waste to Landfill

Intel pioneered the circular economy approach in the semiconductor industry. Our initiatives include:

  • Waste Reduction: 99% of manufacturing waste diverted from landfills
  • Recycled Materials: Increasing use of recycled content in products and packaging
  • Product Design: Engineering products for easy disassembly and material recovery
  • Supply Chain: Working with suppliers to minimize waste throughout the value chain

Circular Innovation

We're developing breakthrough technologies to recover valuable materials from electronic waste, including rare earth elements and precious metals, closing the loop on resource consumption.
